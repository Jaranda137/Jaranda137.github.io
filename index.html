On this website you can get a diagnostic of a total of 5 diseases, breast cancer, diabetes, hepatitis, parkinson and HCC (hepatocellular carcinoma). This diagnostic is done using AI supervised learning algorithms, which can also be chosen. These algorithms use the data proporcioned in the table, and they try to use the patterns seen in the datasets on which they were trained to make a diagnostic of 0 (negative) or 1 (positive).

First of all you need to choose the disease of which you want to make a diagnosis. After that, choose the algorithm that will make the diagnosis, we highly recommend using the algorithm with better performance. Then hit the button predict to see the results. In case of having so much data stored press the button clean tables.

Choose the disease that you want to detect

Breast cancer is a disease that shows up when the cells in the breast start to grow out of control. There are a few types of this disease, which will depend on the cells in the breast that turn into cancer. The breast is composed of different parts, including the lobules, which are the glands that can produce milk; the ducts, which carry the milk to the nipple; and connective tissue, which holds and surrounds everything together.

Growth of malignant cells in breast tissue

The algorithms are trained on Breast Cancer Wisconsin (Diagnostic) Data Set from the UCI Machine Learning repository.

The algorithm that showed better performance on this dataset was Random Forest

Diabetes is a long-lasting and chronic health condition that affects how your body transforms food into energy. Normally, your body breaks down the food that you eat into glucose and releases it into your bloodstream. When your blood glucose level is high, it signals your pancreas to release insulin, which allows your blood to let the blood glucose into your body’s cells for use as energy. The problem with diabetes is that your body doesn’t produce enough insulin or doesn’t use it as well as it should. Consequently, too much blood sugar (glucose) stays in your bloodstream, which can cause serious health problems such as heart disease and vision loss, among others.

Type 2 diabetes mellitus

The algorithms are trained on Pima Indians Diabetes Database posted by UCI MACHINE LEARNING

The algorithm that showed better performance on this dataset was Logistic Regression

Hepatitis is frequently caused by an inflammation of the liver, the principal cause of this disease are normally viruses but it can also be due to other factors such as other infections, heavy alcohol use, toxins, certain medications, and autoimmune disease. Hepatitis A and E are often caused by an ingestion of contaminated water or food, hepatitis B is caused by the contact of infected blood, and other bodily fluids through sex, sharing needles or other drug-injection equipment or from mother to baby at birth. Hepatitis C is a blood-borne virus that is largely spread by sharing needles or other drug injection equipment, and hepatitis D, which is transmitted through contact with infectious blood, occurs only among people with hepatitis B infection.

Microscopy of the liver showing fatty changes, necrosis, and Mallory's bodies.

The algorithms are trained on Hepatitis Dataset from the UCI Machine Learning repository.

The algorithm that showed better performance on this dataset was Logistic Regression

Parkinson's disease (PD) is a progressive neurological disorder that affects movement. It is caused by the degeneration of dopamine-producing neurons in a specific area of the brain. Some of the symptoms of PD include tremors, stiffness, and difficulty with balance and coordination. The disease is chronic, meaning it is ongoing and has no cure, but it can be managed with medication and other therapies. LDP stands for "Levodopa-Responsive Parkinsonism," which refers to a subtype of PD that responds well to treatment with the medication levodopa.

Fluorodeoxyglucose (18F) positron emission tomography (FDG): warmer areas reflect increased glucose uptake; and may reveal impaired dopaminergic activity in the basal ganglia and facilitate diagnosis

The algorithms are trained on Parkinson's Dataset from the UCI Machine Learning repository.

The algorithm that showed better performance on this dataset was Gradient Boosted tree

Hepatocellular carcinoma, or liver cancer, consists of more than 90% of the primary tumor of the liver, it is the fifth most common cause of cancer worldwide, and the second leading cause of cancer death lung cancer in men. About 85% of the people diagnosed with cirrhosis have this disease. Some significant risk factors of hepatocellular carcinoma include viral hepatitis and alcoholic liver disease. It is normally detected during a screening for an underlying disease. Some of the symptoms include: Abdominal discomfort or distention (enlargement), Weight loss, Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes), Gastrointestinal hemorrhage (bleeding), Nausea or vomiting, Persistent itching and Fever.

Hepatocellular carcinoma in an individual who tested positive

The algorithms are trained on HCC Survival Dataset from the UCI Machine Learning repository.

The algorithm that showed better performance on this dataset was Gradient Boosted tree
